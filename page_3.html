Noise: Organic randomness

The Noise TOP is a tool that creates random, but organically distributed, patterns. It's like a digital paintbrush for making swirly, cloudy textures.

Noise is a fundamental part of most 3D artists' toolkit, as it's a single algorithm that applies to a huge amount of creative problems.

In TouchDesigner, Noise has several settings you can adjust;

  1. Noise Type: Choose from options like "Perlin" (smooth, wavy patterns) or "Cell" (more spotty).
  2. Amplitude: Controls how strong or intense the noise pattern is.
  3. Frequency: Adjusts how detailed or zoomed-in the pattern looks.
  4. Offset: Lets you move the pattern around, like sliding a piece of paper.
  5. Seed: Changes the starting point of the random pattern.
  6. Octaves: Adds layers of detail to make the noise more complex.

By playing with these settings, you can create natural-looking effects for things like cloudy backgrounds, rocky surfaces, or wiggly movements. This helps make your visuals feel more organic and less computer-generated.
