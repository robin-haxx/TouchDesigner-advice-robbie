Making Stuff Audio-Reactive in TouchDesigner:
A Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Getting Audio Into TouchDesigner: (CHOPs)

    • Audio File In: Use this to play a specific audio file.
    • Audio Device In: This captures live audio from your computer's microphone or other input devices.

  2. Analyzing the Audio:

    • After getting the audio in, use an Audio Analysis CHOP.
      • This is one of the most useful tools in the Palette.
    • This tool breaks down the audio into useful information like volume, bass, or treble levels.

  3. Using the Analysis:

    • Go to the settings for the Audio Analysis and set the parts of the audio you want to use to be active.
    • Connect the Audio Analysis CHOP to a null. Now you can drag its channels' data other parts of your project.
    • For example, you can use the volume to control the size of a shape, or bass levels to change colours.

  4. Making Things React:

    • Link the audio data to parameters of visual elements.
    • For instance, make a circle pulse with the beat or have things animate faster with bass (lows) or high frequencies, like in p5.js.

Smoothing the Reaction

The audio reaction will likely be quite jumpy. Use a Lag CHOP, after the null, to smooth out the movements of your audio data.

Tip: Try using 0.1 or 0.2 in the first lag parameter, and something like 0.8 in the second one. This creates a snappy "ramp up" but slower "ease off". The idea for this is to make your animation responsive, but controlled and not overwhelming

Starting Simple

Remember, start simple! Maybe begin by just making a shape's size change with the overall volume.
As you get comfortable, you can create more complex stuff.
You don't have to understand the effects you use fully, but the more you focus on using tools you can trace back and solve errors with, the better.
To the same effect, sometimes it's good to remove parts of your project that don't seem to do much - just click the nodes' "->" button on the left first to bypass it and see what your output looks like without it.

Noise with Audio Reactivity

In this example:

  • The Noise's Z axis (3rd translation parameter) is composed of:
    • Audio channel data (edited with Math and Lag CHOPs)
    • Plus absTime.seconds * 0.2 (time in seconds, divided by 5)

Implementation Details

  1. Drag the data for the audio channel in
  2. Manually add " +(absTime.seconds*.2) " to the line of code

This setup means it animates at a constant rate, but this is added upon when the audio level changes.
