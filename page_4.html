Doing colourful and designerly things

Composition and Layout:

Create visual hierarchy: Use size, color, and positioning to guide the viewer's eye through your composition. The rule of thirds can help.

Colour Theory:

  • Colour harmonies: Use complementary, analogous, or triadic color schemes in your projects, or study the style of an artist who appeals to you.
  • Use the HSV (Hue, Saturation, Value) and RGB controls in TOPs to adjust colors dynamically. Think of how animation can shift certain colours.
  • Create mood with color: e.g. warm colours are usually more energetic â€“ colour choice is key to successfully capturing an emotion.

Typography:

  • Use Text TOPs to incorporate typography into your designs.
  • Pay attention to font, size, and spacing and the intent of your text.
  • Animate text properties for dynamic typography.
    There are a lot of options in TD for ways to make dynamic lyric videos, if that interests you.

Negative Space:

  • Don't overcrowd your compositions. Use empty space effectively to create balance and focus.
  • Experiment with masking techniques (e.g. composite TOP -> operation) to create interesting negative space and light blending effects.
    (this works by letting you decide how to combine the colour channel data of the images)
  • I really suggest getting the hang of how to composite with shapes and other visual elements.
    It's a skill that also transfers to how design programs like Photoshop work.

Contrast and Balance:

  • Use contrasting elements (light/dark, large/small) to create visual interest.
  • If in doubt, try to make the canvas look more balanced.

Patterns and Repetition:

  • Use Replicator TOPs or for-each loops to create repeating elements.
  • Look up TD examples for grids, radial arrangements, + pattern designs.

Animation Principles:

  • Apply easing (e.g. lag CHOP) to your animations for natural movement.
  • Use first animation principles, like anticipation and follow-through.

I also suggest planning ideas on paper (as a design student, it's important that you consider the process of your concepts as thoughtfully as possible, and keep a record of how your idea for a design goal starts, and develops over time.)

Keep in mind that knowing what to add or remove to your project to better serve your goals is key to the process - but really, for this brief specifically, interesting output that shows your experimentation is a very valid goal in itself.
